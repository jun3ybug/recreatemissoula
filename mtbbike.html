Mountain Biking


Mountain biking miles of exhilirating trails or cruising around town, you'll quickly discover why Missoula has been dubbed a gold level bike-friendly city. On any given day, you may well see more bikes parked and locked up than cars in downtown. Missoula's bicycle system includes more than 20 miles of bike lanes and routes on major streets. Head ten minutes from the heart of downtown and you can put yourself on a scenic slope surrounded by nothing but nature.

Major mountain biking trail systems are located within minutes of downtown Missoula and include the Rattlesnake Recreation Area, Pattee Canyon, and Blue Mountain. Additionally, the Clark Fork Riverfront Trail System links to biking trails in the Deer Creek and Marshall Mountain areas. If you like singletrack trails in pine forests with magnificent scenic vistas; you’ll love Mountain Biking in Missoula.

When you're out on Missoula's trails, whether in town or in the national forest, remember that you are one of many traveling these pathways. If you meet other people on the trail, be considerate and courteous. It will make everyone's outing safer and more enjoyable.

Show respect for the land, too. After all, Missoula's natural beauty is one of its greatest assets. Trails are one of the best ways to get out and experience our city and its surroundings. Don't let misuse of trails diminish the quality of this opportunity for you and others in the future.
